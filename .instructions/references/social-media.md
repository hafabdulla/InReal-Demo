# Social Media Reference

Use this template for all social media content: LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Instagram, TikTok, Reels, etc.

## The InReal Social Media Register

**Tone:** Conversational, punchy, like talking to a sharp friend at a rooftop bar.
**Length varies by platform:**
- Twitter/X: 280 characters (use the full limit)
- LinkedIn: 130-150 words ideal; up to 2,000 for essays
- Instagram caption: 125-150 words
- TikTok/Reels script: 30-60 seconds (~75-150 words spoken)

**Golden rule:** Every post should be quotable or shareable in isolation.

## Platform-Specific Frameworks

### LinkedIn (B2B + Thought Leadership)

**Structure (for shorter posts):**
```
[Attention hook - question or counter-intuitive statement]
[Body - 2-3 sentences of substance]
[Proof point - number, data, or quote]
[CTA - specific action]
```

**Word count:** 80-130 words (optimal engagement)

**Example:**
"Most real estate investors spend $300k minimum to get started.

We just launched fractional ownership at $500.

Here's what changed: Instead of asking 'Who can afford to buy?' we asked 'Who wants to own real estate but never had access?'

68% of millennials said they'd invest in real estate if they could afford it. The demand was never the problem.

**Result:** $2.4M deployed in our first month.

If real estate access is gatekept, the gate's broken. [Link]"

### X/Twitter (Speed, Authority)

**Structure:**
```
[Hook - provocative or surprising statement]
[Substance - 1-2 sentences max]
[Optional: quote, stat, or why-this-matters]
[Optional: CTA or link]
```

**Word count:** 180-250 characters (leave room for retweets)

**Examples:**
- "You can own real estate in Dubai for $500. No accreditation required. No lawyer fees. No landlord headaches. Just rental income. Here's how. [link]"
- "Real estate investing isn't exclusive. It's just been priced that way. Until now. [link]"
- "The $300 trillion real estate market just opened to people with $500 and an internet connection. You're welcome."

**Best practices:**
- Use 1-2 line breaks for readability
- Include link if driving traffic
- Never use hashtags (they're spam on X)
- Mention != tag (use @ sparingly)

### Instagram (Visual-First, Community)

**Structure:**
```
[Attention hook - emotional or aspirational opening]
[Story or context - 2-3 sentences]
[Proof point - specific detail, number, or benefit]
[CTA - action-oriented and specific]
```

**Word count:** 120-150 words (sweet spot for visibility)

**Caption formula:**

### TikTok / Reels (Hook Hard, Move Fast)

**Structure (for 30-60 second video):**
```
[First 1-2 seconds: Arresting visual + hook line]
[Seconds 3-15: Explanation or demo (simple, clear)]
[Seconds 16-25: Why it matters or the benefit]
[Seconds 26-30: CTA]
```

**Script format (30 seconds ≈ 75 words):**

**Visual:** Text overlay on B-roll (city skyline, apartment interior, phone screen)

**VO/Text:** "Real estate investing cost $300,000 minimum. So we built this. [swipe to phone]"

**Visual:** Phone screen showing $500 investment

**VO/Text:** "Now it costs $500. Same institutional-grade property. Same rental income."

**Visual:** Bar chart showing projected yield

**VO/Text:** "7.8% projected annual yield. Zero landlord work."

**Visual:** CTA button overlay

**VO/Text:** "Own real estate in 2 minutes. [Link in bio]"

---

**Best practices:**
- Hook in the FIRST FRAME (first 1-2 seconds) or lose them
- Use high contrast, movement, text overlay
- Trending audio = higher reach (but only if it fits the brand)
- Captions/text overlay is essential (many watch muted)
- CTA should be clear and specific ("Tap link" not "Learn more")

## Engagement & Response Playbook

**When responding to comments/DMs:**
- Match the tone (casual ↔ professional based on who's asking)
- Answer the question fully in your first response
- Use the person's first name when appropriate
- Link to specific next steps (app, property page, email signup)
- Never be defensive; always add information

**Red flags to avoid:**
- Hyperbolic claims ("Make passive income fast!")
- Guaranteed returns
- "FOMO" language ("Limited time!" "Act now!")
- Vague promises ("Life-changing wealth-building opportunity")

## Metrics That Matter for InReal Content

**Track these (not vanity metrics like likes):**
- **Click-through rate** (% who click the link)
- **Saves** (indicates it was valuable enough to revisit)
- **Shares** (indicates it was worth spreading)
- **Comments with questions** (indicates engagement with content, not just brand)
- **Link clicks leading to app install**
- **Conversion rate** (visitors → verified users)

Low engagement ≠ bad content. Bad conversion ≠ bad content. **Track the behavior that actually matters: do people invest after seeing this?**
