# Investor Narrative Reference

Use this for all investor-facing materials: pitch deck narratives, investor letters, LP updates, funding announcements, data room cover memos, and financial storytelling.

## The InReal Investor Narrative Register

**Tone:** Data-dense, precise, like Sequoia's investor letters.
**Principle:** Substance over story. Numbers prove the narrative.
**Goal:** Demonstrate market size, unit economics, and execution.

SLIDE 14: RISK MITIGATION
HEADLINE: "How We Manage Downside Scenarios"

SCENARIO 1: Tenant Default
Risk: Investor doesn't get distributions
Mitigation: 
- Reserve fund covers 6 months of defaults
- Tenant screening: top 20% credit scores
- Insurance on 100% of portfolio
- Result: 0.08% historical default rate

SCENARIO 2: Regulatory Risk
Risk: Market (e.g., Dubai, Singapore) tightens foreign ownership rules
Mitigation:
- We partner with local legal counsel in each market
- Deal structure is registered with local regulators
- Diversification across 3+ markets reduces single-market risk

SCENARIO 3: Liquidity Risk
Risk: Investors can't exit; product fails
Mitigation:
- Secondary market allows anytime sales (1–4 weeks typical)
- Not promised liquidity (like REITs), but built-in exit
- Investor education: "This is real estate, not day trading"

SCENARIO 4: Market Downturn
Risk: Property values drop; investors take losses
Mitigation:
- Real estate is a long-hold asset (20+ years historically)
- Rental income (7-8% yield) covers drawdowns in most cases
- Investor diversification: one investor owns 3–5 properties across markets

NARRATIVE:
"We're a real estate company, not a tech company hiding real estate risk. Real estate has real risk. We manage it through reserve funds, diversification, and transparent disclosure. We don't promise returns; we show the data and let investors decide."

## Investor FAQ

**Anticipate and answer common questions before they're asked.**

```
Q: What's your deal sourcing strategy? Can you actually find 50 properties/year?
A: We have three sourcing channels:
1. Developer partnerships (bulk, pre-development)
2. Institutional sellers (REITs downsizing, corporate property disposals)
3. BTS (build-to-suit) new construction

We've signed LOIs with 5 developers representing 200+ units in pipeline. Our bottleneck is capital, not deal flow.

Q: What happens if a market's regulations change mid-investment?
A: Each investor agreement specifies regulatory exit rights. If a market becomes unfriendly to foreign ownership, we unwind positions at then-current value, or investors choose to hold. We've modeled this scenario; Singapore is 15% of AUM (diversified risk).

Q: What's the default risk? What if tenant doesn't pay?
A: Historical default: 0.08%. Our reserve fund covers 6 months of portfolio-wide default. We also use property insurance for catastrophic scenarios. Investor capital is not at risk from single defaults.

Q: How do you make money if the property goes down in value?
A: We make money on management fees (1.5% of AUM), not appreciation. If a property decreases in value, our fee also decreases, so we're aligned with downside risk. This incentivizes conservative sourcing.

Q: Why not just buy REITs?
A: REITs are more liquid but less transparent and have lower yields (typically 3-5% vs. our 7-8% in emerging markets). We target emerging markets with higher rental yields because that's where supply is constrained and tenant demand is highest.

Q: How liquid is secondary market? What if I need money?
A: Secondary market is not guaranteed liquid (it's real estate, not stocks). Typical timeline: 1-4 weeks to find a buyer. We publish bid-ask spreads weekly so investors can see what the market is pricing their positions at. This is not a day-trading vehicle; it's a long-hold real estate play with optional early exit.

Q: What's your path to profitability?
A: We break even at ~$30M AUM (67 properties). We're currently at $52M AUM with positive unit economics. EBITDA positive by Q2 2025 without Series B capital. Series A capital goes to expansion, not runway.
```

## Investor Narrative Checklist

Before sending any investor material:

- [ ] **Every financial claim has a source:** Numbers, not adjectives.
- [ ] **Unit economics are crystal clear:** How do you make money per property? Per investor?
- [ ] **Risk is transparent:** Not hidden, not minimized. Stated clearly.
- [ ] **Projections are conservative:** Would an institutional investor believe them? (Not optimistic fantasy.)
- [ ] **Competitive positioning is honest:** Why you, not 10 other startups building similar things?
- [ ] **Team credentials are relevant:** Founders have actually done this before, not just read articles.
- [ ] **Use of funds is specific:** Every dollar is allocated to something measurable (properties, tech, hiring).
