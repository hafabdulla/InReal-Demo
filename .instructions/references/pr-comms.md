# PR & Communications Reference

Use this for all public-facing announcements: press releases, media pitches, crisis communications, partner announcements, investor updates, and press statements.

## The InReal PR Register

**Tone:** Factual, quotable, like the press release Reuters would actually run.
**Principle:** Facts first. Quotes enhance, not substitute.
**Goal:** Coverage that moves your market, not just ego.

---

## Press Release Structure

**Total length:** 300–500 words (journalists skim; edit ruthlessly).

**Format:**

```
[FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE]

[HEADLINE] 
40-60 characters. Newsy, not promotional.
"InReal Launches Fractional Ownership in Singapore Properties"
NOT: "Revolutionary Platform Disrupts Real Estate Industry!"

[SUBHEADING - optional]
Clarifies the headline. Max 25 words.
"$4M in property assets now available to investors starting at $500"

[DATELINE]
[CITY], [DATE]

[OPENING PARAGRAPH - The Lead]
Who? What? When? Where? Why?
Must answer all 5 in 2-3 sentences.
"InReal, a platform for fractional real estate ownership, today announced its expansion to Singapore with three institutional-grade properties totaling $4 million in assets under management. The platform, which enables investors to begin with $500, now operates across Bangkok, Dubai, and Singapore. (5 years old | 1,200 investors | $24M deployed globally)"

[BODY PARAGRAPH 1 - The Details]
What does this mean? What can users/investors do?
"The Singapore properties include a 2-bedroom in Marina Bay area with 6.9% projected yield and a 3-bedroom in District 9 with 7.1% yield. All properties are fully tenanted and professionally managed. Investors can view the properties immediately in the InReal app."

[BODY PARAGRAPH 2 - The Context]
Why is this happening? What market trend does it reflect?
"Singapore's rental market has seen 18% appreciation over the past 3 years, driven by expat demand and limited new supply. Traditional real estate ownership remains prohibitively expensive for most first-time investors, with entry prices averaging $450,000 for a 2-bedroom in prime rental areas."

[BODY PARAGRAPH 3 - Optional: Third-Party Validation]
A quote from a partner, regulator, or expert (not InReal).
"'We've seen increasing demand from global investors for exposure to Asian real estate without the $300k entry barrier,' said [External Expert Name], [Title], [Affiliation]."

[QUOTE 1 - InReal CEO or Founder]
20-30 words. Memorable, not corporate.
"Singapore has always been the gold standard for Asian real estate. Now, you don't need $500k to own a piece of it." — [Name], CEO, InReal

[BODY PARAGRAPH 4 - Call to Action (Soft)]
How can journalists, investors, or users learn more?
"Investors can browse the new Singapore properties immediately in the InReal app. Media inquiries: [press@inreal.com]"

[ABOUT INREAL - Standard Boilerplate]
2-3 sentences. Don't change this between releases.
"InReal is a BVI-regulated platform enabling fractional ownership of institutional-grade real estate. Since [Year], InReal has deployed $24M across 1,200+ investors in Bangkok, Dubai, and Singapore. [Website]"

[MEDIA CONTACT]
Name, email, phone
"[Name] | Communications Manager | InReal | [email] | [phone]"

[###]
```

## Crisis Communication Template

**When something goes wrong: tenant default, property damage, regulatory issue, etc.**

**Principle:** Respond fast. Be transparent. Describe action taken, not just the problem.

```
SUBJECT: Update on [Property Name] — Tenant Situation

Dear InReal Investors,

On [Date], we identified that the tenant at our Bangkok Marina property (where 50+ of you have invested) was 2 months behind on rent.

Here's what happened and what we're doing:

WHAT HAPPENED:
The tenant, a mid-market company, experienced cash flow issues due to [context]. They informed us on [date] and committed to a repayment plan.

WHAT WE'VE DONE:
✓ Legal team initiated formal eviction proceedings on [date]
✓ We've secured a replacement tenant effective [date] 
✓ Interim distributions will be paid on schedule; the difference will be made up from our reserve fund

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU:
- Your August distribution will be paid in full on Sept 5 (normal schedule)
- The property will be back to full tenancy on [date]
- You will not experience any loss or delay in distributions due to this default

WHAT WE'VE LEARNED:
This is rare (0.1% of our portfolio). But it revealed a gap: our tenant screening didn't catch early warning signs. We've upgraded our process and our reserve fund to cover 6 months of defaults across the portfolio.

This is transparency in action. We don't hide problems; we fix them and tell you.

Questions? Reply to this email or schedule a call.

Best,
[Name], CEO
```

## Press Release Checklist

Before publishing:

- [ ] **Headline passes the newspaper test:** Would this appear above the fold in a business publication?
- [ ] **Facts are sourced:** Every claim is backed by data or a quote from someone authorized to speak.
- [ ] **Quotes are memorable:** Would a journalist actually quote this in an article? If not, cut it.
- [ ] **No hype language:** "Revolutionary," "disruptive," "industry-changing" = deleted.
- [ ] **Specifics over abstractions:** Numbers, dates, names > vague adjectives.
- [ ] **Boilerplate is boring:** That's intentional. It's not the story.
- [ ] **Distribution is planned:** Where will this go? Media outlets? Wire services? Your own channels?

---

## Media Relations Best Practices

**Building relationships that result in coverage:**

1. **Be the story, not the press release.** Journalists want trends, data, human interest. Provide that first; the company mention is secondary.
2. **Source matters.** A quote from an external expert > a quote from your CEO. Both is better.
3. **Timing is everything.** Is there a news hook? Funding round? Market trend? Without it, it's just advertising.
4. **One ask per story.** Don't send 10 pitches at once. If a journalist is interested, one follow-up email is enough.
5. **Respect their time.** A 30-second pitch is better than a 2-minute call without an appointment.
