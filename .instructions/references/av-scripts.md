# Video & Audio Scripts Reference

Use this for all audio-visual content: explainer videos, testimonial scripts, podcast intros, pitch videos, YouTube scripts, Zoom webinars, and any spoken word content.

## The InReal AV Register

**Tone:** Natural, energetic, like a TED talk, not a webinar.
**Principle:** Write for the ear, not the eye. Every sentence should sound good spoken aloud.
**Goal:** Hold attention, explain clearly, move to action.

---

## Explainer Video (2–3 Minutes)

**Goal:** Answer "What is InReal?" for first-time viewers.

**Structure:**
1. **Hook** (0–5 seconds): Establish the problem
2. **Solution** (6–30 seconds): Introduce InReal
3. **How it works** (31–90 seconds): Step through the process
4. **Social proof** (91–120 seconds): Build credibility
5. **CTA** (121–150 seconds): Drive action

## Script Writing Checklist

**Read it aloud before recording:**

- [ ] **Does it flow naturally?** No tongue-twisters or awkward phrasing.
- [ ] **Pacing feels right?** Average speaking pace is 150 words/minute. Script shouldn't feel rushed or slow.
- [ ] **Contractions are used:** "I'm," "We've," "You'll" (more natural than "I am").
- [ ] **Sentences are short:** Average 10-15 words. Long sentences are hard to deliver naturally.
- [ ] **Key claims are specific:** Numbers, not adjectives.
- [ ] **Risk disclosure is clear:** Any mention of returns includes risk language spoken aloud.
- [ ] **CTA is explicit:** No ambiguity about what the viewer should do next.
- [ ] **Tone matches content:** Educational feels different from sales.

## Video Production Notes

### B-Roll / Visual Elements

**For each script section, identify what should be on screen:**

```
SCRIPT: "Real estate requires $300k minimum."
VISUAL: Property listing showing price, or wealthy person with "expensive property" aesthetic

SCRIPT: "InReal fractionalizes ownership."
VISUAL: One building → splits into pieces → each highlighted as a separate investment

SCRIPT: "1,200 investors deployed $3.4M."
VISUAL: Map showing properties in Bangkok, Dubai, Singapore with money/investor icons

SCRIPT: "You earn rental income monthly."
VISUAL: Phone notification showing distribution, then bank account update
```

### Music & Sound Design

**Tone should match:**
- Explainer video: Upbeat, modern, positive (70-90 BPM)
- Testimonial: Warm, intimate, conversational (no overpowering music)
- Pitch deck: Professional, confident, minimal music (focus on data)
- Educational content: Subtle background music (doesn't distract)

**Avoid:**
- Over-dramatic music
- Clichéd "tech startup" sounds
- Music that's louder than the voiceover

### Recording Quality

- **Microphone:** Good USB condenser or lavalier (Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica AT2020, or better)
- **Audio:** Record in a quiet room (avoid echoes). Post-production: normalize levels, remove background noise.
- **Pacing:** Record at a natural speaking pace. Don't rush. You can always edit slower sections.
- **Delivery:** Treat it like a conversation with a friend, not a robot reading a script. Inflection matters.

---

## Accent / Localization Notes

**When recording for international audiences:**

- **Pronunciation:** Write out any local terms phonetically (Bangkok = "BUNG-kok," not "Bong-kong")
- **Currency:** Specify SGD vs. USD vs. THB (don't assume)
- **Cultural references:** Avoid US-centric examples. Use global references.
- **Speed:** Speak slower than you think is natural. International audiences need processing time.
