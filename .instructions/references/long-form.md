# Long-Form Content Reference

Use this for blog posts, thought leadership articles, whitepapers, case studies, market reports, and deep-dive guides.

## The InReal Long-Form Register

**Tone:** Authoritative, generous, like a Bloomberg column but warmer.
**Purpose:** Establish authority, build trust, drive organic reach.
**Principle:** Substance over style. Density over flashiness.

---

## Blog Post Structure (1,000–2,500 words)

### Opening (100-150 words)
**Goal:** Answer "why should I read this?"

```
[Hook: specific problem or counter-intuitive fact]
[Why it matters: concrete stakes]
[What you'll learn: 3-4 clear takeaways]
[Word count promise: "5-minute read" or "8-minute read"]
```

**Example opening:**

"You've been paying rent for 12 years. Your landlord bought the property for $200k in 2010; it's now worth $480k. You've paid him $180k in rent. He's paid $80k in taxes and maintenance. His net: $400k. Your net: $0. This isn't an accident. It's how wealth concentration works. And it's why 78% of millennials say they'd invest in real estate if they could afford it. They can. Just not the way you think."

### Body (800-2,200 words)
**Organize into 4-6 sections. Each section:**
- Starts with a subheading (specific, not generic)
- Opens with a problem or data point
- Provides 2-3 concrete examples or evidence
- Closes with a "so what?"

**Section template:**
```
SUBHEADING: [Benefit or insight, not a placeholder like "Section 2"]

[Data point or problem that grabs attention]

[Explanation with 2-3 examples]

[Proof: case study, stat, third-party validation]

[So what? Why does this matter to the reader?]
```

**Example sections for "Why Bangkok's Real Estate Market Just Opened to Foreigners":**

Section 1: "Thailand's New Foreign Ownership Rules (and Why 2024 Changed Everything)"
Section 2: "What This Means for Your Returns (Spoiler: Rental Yields Just Got Better)"
Section 3: "How Fractional Ownership Simplifies Cross-Border Real Estate"
Section 4: "The Tax Reality (What You Actually Owe)"
Section 5: "Three Investors Share Why They Moved Money to Bangkok"

### Closing (150-250 words)
**Goal:** Convert or deepen engagement.

```
[Recap: 1-2 sentence summary of the big idea]
[So what? Why readers should care going forward]
[Bridge to action: specific next step]
[Soft CTA: not pushy, but clear]
```

**Example closing:**

"Real estate wealth-building didn't break. Access did. That's changing—not for everyone with $300k, but for anyone with $500 and an internet connection. If you've been waiting for real estate to be accessible, it is. The question isn't whether you can afford it anymore. It's whether you will."

**[CTA: Explore Bangkok properties or Download our market guide]**

## Thought Leadership Article (1,500–3,000 words)

**Goal:** Position InReal as a market authority and thought partner.

**Structure:**
1. **Provocative headline** that challenges conventional wisdom
2. **Data-heavy opening** (lead with numbers)
3. **The contrarian argument** (what most people get wrong)
4. **Why it matters** (stakes for the reader)
5. **Evidence & counterarguments** (prove your point is real, not hype)
6. **The implications** (where this is heading)
7. **Call to curiosity** (not a hard sell)

**Example:**

**HEADLINE:** "The $7 Trillion Real Estate Gap (And Why It's About to Close)"

**OPENING:**
"Between 2020 and 2023, real estate prices in Singapore climbed 47%. Bangkok, 52%. Dubai, 38%. Meanwhile, first-time buyer participation dropped 34% in developed markets. The disconnect isn't new. But it's about to break something."

**THE CONTRARIAN ARGUMENT:**
"Most people blame interest rates or supply. Wrong. The real problem: wealth concentration in real estate accelerated precisely when access stopped. For 70 years, real estate was the wealth-builder for the middle class. Then three things happened..."

**WHY IT MATTERS:**
"This has consequences. Demographics show 68% of millennials want real estate exposure. Traditional finance shows zero path for them. So where's the capital going? Nowhere. That's $7 trillion of unrealized wealth transfer sitting on the sidelines."

## Case Study / Success Story Template (800–1,500 words)

**Structure:**
1. **The situation** (before): specific problem, stakes, constraint
2. **The catalyst** (why they tried InReal)
3. **The results** (specific numbers, timeline)
4. **The quote** (emotional insight from the investor)
5. **The lesson** (what this teaches readers)

**Example:**

```
HEADLINE: "From Paying Rent to Earning It: How Sarah Went from $0 Real Estate Exposure to $5,000 Portfolio in 30 Days"

THE SITUATION:
Sarah, 31, lived in Singapore. She'd been paying $1,800/month rent for 5 years ($108k total).
She wanted to invest in real estate. But Singapore's property market required $300k minimum.
She had $8k savings. She felt locked out.

THE CATALYST:
A LinkedIn post about InReal mentioned you could start at $500.
She was skeptical (too good to be true) but curious.
She opened an account. 8 minutes.

THE RESULTS:
Week 1: Invested $500 in a Bangkok property (7.2% projected yield)
Week 2: Added $1,500 to a Dubai listing
Week 3: Allocated $2,000 to a Singapore property
Week 4: Received her first distribution ($18.34 in rental income from Bangkok)

By month 2, she had $5,000 deployed across 3 properties, 3 markets, yielding $47/month in rental income.

THE QUOTE:
"I thought I had to wait until I was 45 and had $300k to be a real estate investor. Turns out I just needed to start. The $500 was low enough that I wasn't afraid. That's the genius of it."

THE LESSON:
The barrier to real estate wasn't financial sophistication.
It was access.
Sarah had the same capital ($8k) she'd always had.
But for the first time, she could deploy it into real estate.
And she did. In a month.
```

## Whitepaper / Deep-Dive Guide (3,000–7,000 words)

**Example topic:** "The Investor's Guide to Bangkok Real Estate: Tax Implications, Regulatory Framework, and Yield Strategy"

**Structure:**
1. **Executive Summary** (250-300 words)
2. **Table of Contents** (shows depth)
3. **Chapter 1: The Market Context** (1,000 words)
4. **Chapter 2: Legal & Tax Landscape** (1,200 words)
5. **Chapter 3: Financial Analysis** (1,000 words)
6. **Chapter 4: Risk Considerations** (800 words)
7. **Chapter 5: Case Study / Real Examples** (800 words)
8. **Appendix: Resources & Further Reading** (200 words)

**Tone:** Authoritative, data-dense, but still readable.

**Every claim should be sourced.** Use footnotes for regulatory citations, market data, tax references.

| Market insights | 1-2x/month | Thought leadership, authority | High (evergreen keywords) |

## SEO Best Practices for Long-Form

**Title:**
- 50-60 characters
- Include main keyword (what problem are you solving?)
- Be specific, not clickbaity

```
Bad: "Real Estate Investing Strategies"
Good: "Bangkok Real Estate for Expats: Tax Guide + Yield Analysis (2024)"
```

**Meta description:**
- 155-160 characters
- Should answer the search intent
- Include a number if possible

```
"Complete guide to investing in Bangkok property as an expat. Tax treatment, yields, regulatory requirements, and how to get started."
```

**Headings hierarchy:**
- 1 H1 per page (the title)
- Multiple H2s (section headers, 5-8 per article)
- H3s for sub-sections (optional but good for complex topics)

**Internal linking:**
- Link to 2-4 related InReal pages/resources
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- Example: "Learn more about our [cross-border investment structure]"

**Keywords:**
- Target 1 primary keyword + 3-5 related keywords
- Don't force keywords; they should appear naturally
- Include variations ("real estate investing," "property investment," "fractional ownership")

## Length Guidelines by Format

| Format | Ideal Length | Reason |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Blog post (educational) | 1,200–2,000 words | Long enough for depth, short enough to read in 8 minutes |
| Blog post (opinion/thought) | 800–1,200 words | Conviction doesn't need length |
| How-to guide | 1,500–3,000 words | Comprehensiveness matters here |
| Market report | 2,500–4,000 words | Data density justifies length |
| Whitepaper | 4,000–8,000 words | Deep credibility play |
| Case study | 800–1,500 words | The story is the value |

## Long-Form Copy Editing Checklist

- [ ] **The headline would work as a tweet:** Is it compelling in 280 characters?
- [ ] **First paragraph can stand alone:** If someone only reads the first paragraph, do they get the main idea?
- [ ] **Subheadings are specific:** They describe the actual content, not just "Section 2."
- [ ] **Data is cited:** Every stat has a source or qualifier (e.g., "based on 2024 market data" or "according to the Bangkok Real Estate Board").
- [ ] **Examples are concrete:** You've named specific properties, investors, or timeframes when possible.
- [ ] **Sentences are readable aloud:** No sentence exceeds 20-25 words on average.
- [ ] **Paragraphs are 3-5 sentences max:** Long paragraphs kill engagement on screens.
- [ ] **Passive voice is eliminated:** Find 5+ instances of passive voice and rewrite in active.
- [ ] **Adjectives earn their place:** Every adjective ("strong," "unique," "compelling") carries information, not just emphasis.
- [ ] **Transitions exist:** You shouldn't need to re-read to follow the logic from section to section.
- [ ] **The closing bridges to action:** Readers know what to do next without guessing.
